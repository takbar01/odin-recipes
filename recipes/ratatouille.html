Ratatouille

In the movie Ratatouille, the scene where Anton Ego eats ratatouille is a pivotal moment in the story. As he takes the first bite, the film takes the viewer on a journey through his memories, back to his childhood and the comfort he found in his mother's homemeade ratatouille. The flavors and textures of the dish are so harmonious that he is left speechless.

VEGGIES

  • 2 eggplants
  • 6 roma tomatoes
  • 2 yellow squashes
  • 2 zucchinis

SAUCE

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium white onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded, and diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, seeded and diced
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • 28 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp thinly sliced fresh basil

HERB SEASONING

  • 2 tbsp thinly sliced fresh basil
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • kosher salt
  • black pepper
  • 4 tbsp olive oil

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit
  2. Cut eggplant, tomatoes, squash, and zucchini into 1-mm thick rounds, then set aside
  3. Make the sauce:
    1. Heat olive oil in a 12 in pan over med-hi heat.
    2. Saut√© the onion, garlic, and bell peppers until soft
    3. Season with salt and pepper
    4. Add the crushed tomatoes
    5. Stir until ingredients are fully incorporated
    6. Remove from heat, then add basil.
    7. Stire once more, then smooth the surface of the sauce with a spatula
  4. Arrange veggies in alternating slices on top of the sauce
  5. Season with salt and pepper
  6. Make the herb seasoning by mixing together the basil, garlic, parsley, thyme, salt, pepper, and olive oil
  7. Spoon the herb seasoning over the vegetables
  8. Serve hot as a main dish or side
  9. Enjoy!
